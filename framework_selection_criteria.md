# Criteria for AI Agent Framework Selection

This document outlines the criteria for selecting a popular and relevant Python AI agent framework for Outlast Solutions. The criteria are divided into two main categories: Popularity & Community Health, and Relevance & Applicability.

---

### 1. Popularity & Community Health Criteria

These criteria measure the framework's public traction, stability, and the robustness of its support ecosystem. A popular framework is more likely to be well-maintained, secure, and have a larger pool of developers.

*   **GitHub Activity & Metrics**
    *   **Description**: A quantitative measure of the project's health and adoption on GitHub. Key indicators include GitHub stars (general interest), forks (community contribution interest), number of contributors, and the frequency of recent commits (active development).
    *   **Relevance**: High activity suggests a living, evolving project that is less likely to be abandoned. A large number of contributors indicates a strong and diverse development base.

*   **Community Support & Engagement**
    *   **Description**: The availability and activity level of community support channels such as official forums, Discord/Slack servers, and Stack Overflow tags. 
    *   **Relevance**: A vibrant community is crucial for troubleshooting, sharing best practices, and reducing developer friction. It ensures that help is available when developers encounter issues.

*   **Project Maturity & Adoption**
    *   **Description**: The age of the project, the stability of its releases, and its adoption by other reputable companies or projects.
    *   **Relevance**: A mature framework is more likely to be stable and have fewer critical bugs. Adoption by others serves as a vote of confidence in the framework's capabilities and long-term viability.

### 2. Relevance & Applicability Criteria

These criteria assess the framework's suitability for Outlast Solutions' specific technical and business requirements.

*   **Documentation Quality**
    *   **Description**: The clarity, completeness, and organization of the official documentation. This includes API references, tutorials, conceptual guides, and practical examples.
    *   **Relevance**: High-quality documentation is essential for reducing the learning curve and enabling developers to be productive quickly. It is a direct indicator of the project's commitment to user experience.

*   **Ease of Use & Developer Experience**
    *   **Description**: The simplicity and intuitiveness of the framework's API and core abstractions. This assesses how quickly a developer can build, test, and debug a functional agent.
    *   **Relevance**: A framework with a good developer experience accelerates development cycles, simplifies maintenance, and makes it easier to onboard new team members.

*   **Applicability to Outlast Solutions Use Cases**
    *   **Description**: The framework's built-in capabilities or extensibility to address key Outlast Solutions use cases, including:
        *   **Data Processing & Automation**: Automating data ingestion, transformation, and analysis pipelines.
        *   **Intelligent Automation**: Creating agents that can perform complex, multi-step tasks across different systems.
        *   **Customer Support Bots**: Building and deploying conversational AI agents for customer interaction.
    *   **Relevance**: The chosen framework must directly support or be easily adaptable to our core business needs to ensure a high return on investment.
